Community insights

Gundaroo Common Community Survey 2025

A visual summary of the Gundaroo community survey conducted in June 2025 to inform the future management of the Common.

Download the full report (PDF)

1. How many people live in your household?

The survey received 163 household responses, representing 499 individuals—around 40% of Gundaroo’s population based on the 2021 ABS census.

2. Where does your household reside?

60% of responding households (98) live within the 2003 gazetted 'Commoners boundary' (Zone A), with the remaining 40% located in the expanded village and surrounding district. Following the survey, and at the request of the Gundaroo Common Trust, the NSW Government expanded the ‘commoners’ boundary’ to encompass the whole of the modern Gundaroo village.

3. Before today, were you or your household aware of our village Common?

Awareness is universal: 100% of respondents already knew about the Gundaroo Common before taking the survey.

4. Is anyone in your household a Commoner?

18% of households (30) reported that someone is on the roll of Commoners, while another 15% (24 households) were unsure, pointing to an opportunity to improve communication about Commoner status.

5. In an average month, how often would you or someone from your household visit the Common?

The most common pattern was visiting 2–3 times per month (30%). A further 38% of households visit at least weekly, and only 7% said they never visit.

6. Which activities have you or your household engaged in at the Common?

Households make wide use of the Common. The leading activities were Walking (124 responses), Nature appreciation (99), and Dog walking (88).

7. Select your household’s primary or most frequent use of the Common.

When asked to choose a single primary use, Walking dominated with 64 households, reinforcing the Common’s role as a recreational greenspace.

8. How would you rate the overall value of the Common to you or your household?

The Common is highly valued: 69% of households gave it the maximum 5 out of 5, and the overall average rating was 4.4/5.

9. If you value the Common, what are the main reasons?

The top reasons were Recreation and exercise (81%), followed by Biodiversity (72%) and the Common’s historical significance (56%). Residents value the Common not just as a place for recreation, but as an area of significant natural and cultural heritage that should be preserved.

10. On a scale of 1–5, how important is the Common to your overall sense of community?

The Common is a meaningful part of Gundaroo's social fabric. The average rating was 4.4/5, with 61% of respondents selecting the maximum score of 5.

11. Would you consider donating money to support the management of the Common?

The community's value translates into strong tangible support; 82% of households (133)indicated they would consider donating money to help manage the Common.

12. Additional comments or suggestions

67 responses included comments. Thematic analysis highlighted eight recurring themes, led by General appreciation & identity (37%), along with Funding & governance, Education & community engagement, and Access, signage & infrastructure (each 18%).
